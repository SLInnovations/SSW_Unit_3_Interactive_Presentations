Learn
Why Variety Matters

Compare these two paragraphs about the same topic:

❌ BORING — All Declarative
Dogs are great pets. Dogs are loyal and friendly. Dogs need exercise every day. Dogs can learn many tricks. Dogs make people happy.
✓ VARIED — Mixed Sentence Types
Have you ever had a dog? Dogs are amazing pets! They are loyal, friendly, and always happy to see you. Take your dog for a walk every day. Did you know they can learn over 100 tricks? A dog will bring so much joy into your life.

Same topic, but the varied paragraph uses interrogative, exclamatory, declarative, and imperative sentences to keep the reader engaged.

Learn
Strategies for Each Type

Use these tips when writing each sentence type:

📢 DECLARATIVE: State facts, share information, or give opinions. Most sentences in your writing will be declarative.
❓ INTERROGATIVE: Ask your reader a question to pull them in. Great for openings and transitions.
😲 EXCLAMATORY: Show genuine excitement or surprise. Use sparingly — too many loses their impact.
👑 IMPERATIVE: Give advice or instructions. Works well in persuasive and informational writing.

Tricky Concepts
Tips for Great Variety
Don't Overdo Exclamatory Sentences
If every sentence ends with an exclamation point, none of them feel special. Save exclamatory sentences for moments of genuine strong feeling.
Use Questions to Hook Your Reader
Starting a paragraph with an interrogative sentence is a powerful way to grab your reader's attention and make them want to keep reading.
Most Sentences Will Be Declarative
That is perfectly fine. The goal is not to use every type equally — it is to break up the pattern so your writing flows naturally.
