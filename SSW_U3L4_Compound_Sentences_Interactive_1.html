Learn
What Is a Compound Sentence?
Two complete sentences joined together.
A compound sentence is two independent clauses (complete sentences) joined with a comma and a FANBOYS conjunction.
THE FORMULA
Independent Clause + , + FANBOYS + Independent Clause
Example
The sun was shining, but the wind was cold.
Two complete sentences joined with , but
Learn
The Comma Rule
When do you need a comma before a conjunction?
✓ Comma Needed
"I was hungry, so I made a sandwich."
Both parts are complete sentences.
✗ No Comma Needed
"I was hungry and tired."
"Tired" alone is NOT a complete sentence.
🔍 The Comma Test
Step 1: Find the conjunction.
Step 2: Cover everything BEFORE the conjunction.
Step 3: Is what's LEFT a complete sentence?
If YES → Use a comma before the conjunction.
If NO → No comma needed.

Tricky
Common Mistakes
Three errors to watch out for.
🚫 Mistake 1: Forgetting the Comma
✗ "I was tired but I kept working."
✓ "I was tired, but I kept working."
🚫 Mistake 2: Comma Splice (No Conjunction)
✗ "I was tired, I kept working."
✓ "I was tired, but I kept working."
A comma alone cannot join two complete sentences.
🚫 Mistake 3: Unnecessary Comma
✗ "I like dogs, and cats."
✓ "I like dogs and cats."
"Cats" is not a complete sentence, so no comma is needed.
