Every Sentence Has a Job

Just like people have different jobs, sentences have different jobs too. There are four types:

📢
Declarative
Makes a statement (tells something)
Ends with a period (.)
Think: DECLARE = announce
❓
Interrogative
Asks a question
Ends with a question mark (?)
Think: INTERROGATE = ask
😲
Exclamatory
Shows strong feeling
Ends with an exclamation point (!)
Think: EXCLAIM = cry out
👑
Imperative
Gives a command or request
Ends with a period (.) or (!)
Think: EMPEROR = gives orders
Learn
One Topic, Four Sentence Types

Watch how we can write about the same topic using all four types:

DECLARATIVE: The CN Tower is located in Toronto.
INTERROGATIVE: Have you ever visited the CN Tower?
EXCLAMATORY: The view from the CN Tower is breathtaking!
IMPERATIVE: Visit the CN Tower when you go to Toronto.

Tricky Concepts
Watch Out for These
Imperative vs. Exclamatory
"Stop right there!" looks exclamatory because of the exclamation point, but it is imperative — it gives a command. The exclamation point just shows urgency.
Hidden Subjects in Imperatives
Imperative sentences often have a hidden subject: "you." When someone says "Open the window," they mean "(You) open the window."
Ask: What Is the Sentence's Job?
When you are unsure, ask: "What is this sentence doing?" Is it telling? Asking? Showing strong feeling? Or commanding?
