Turning Choppy into Smooth

Learn
What Is Choppy Writing?
Too many short sentences in a row sounds robotic.
✗ Choppy
"I went to the store. I bought apples. I bought bananas. I bought milk. I came home. I put the groceries away."
✓ Smooth
"I went to the store and bought apples, bananas, and milk. Then I came home and put the groceries away."
Same information, much better flow. Good writers combine related ideas.
Learn
Three Combining Strategies
Choose the right tool for the job.
1
Use a Conjunction
"I was tired. I kept working." →
"I was tired, but I kept working."
Join two complete ideas with comma + FANBOYS.
2
Combine Repeated Parts
"I bought apples. I bought bananas." →
"I bought apples and bananas."
Remove the repeated words, connect with AND.
3
Move Details into One Sentence
"The dog was big. The dog was brown. The dog barked." →
"The big, brown dog barked."
Combine descriptions into one sentence.

Tricky
When NOT to Combine
Sometimes short sentences are powerful on purpose.
🎯 Short Sentences Create Impact
Powerful: "The room fell silent. Everyone stared. Nobody moved."

Weaker: "The room fell silent, and everyone stared, and nobody moved."

The short sentences create tension and drama. Combining them removes the impact.
⚖️ The Balance Rule
Good writing uses a mix of sentence lengths. Combine to fix choppiness, but keep short sentences when they add emphasis or drama. Not every sentence needs to be long.
🚫 Watch Out for Over-Combining
Too much: "I went to the store and bought apples and bananas and milk and then I came home and put everything away and started making dinner."

This sentence tries to do too much. Break it into 2 or 3 sentences instead.
