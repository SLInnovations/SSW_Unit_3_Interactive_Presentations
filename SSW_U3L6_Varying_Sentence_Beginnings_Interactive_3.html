Varying Sentence Beginnings
Starting Sentences in Different Ways
Today's Goal: I can start my sentences in different ways to avoid repetition and make my writing more interesting.
Sentence
Beginnings
Same start every time = flat writing. Variety = engaging writing.

Learn
The Problem: Same Beginnings
When every sentence starts the same way, writing sounds flat.
✗ Repetitive
The dog ran across the yard. The dog chased a squirrel. The dog barked loudly. The dog wagged his tail. The dog came back inside.
✓ Varied
The dog ran across the yard. Excitedly, he barked at the squirrel from below. Meanwhile, his tail wagged with excitement. Tired and happy, he finally came back inside.
Editing trick: Circle the first word of every sentence. Too many repeats? Time to revise.
Learn
Five Ways to Start a Sentence
Same idea, five different openings.
1
Subject (the usual way)
"The dog barked at the mailman."
2
Adverb
"Quietly, the cat crept across the room."
Tells how, when, or where. Comma after.
3
Where/When Phrase
"In the morning, we packed our bags."
A group of words telling where or when. Comma after.
4
Adjective / Describing Phrase
"Tired and hungry, the hikers reached camp."
Describing words about the subject. Comma after.
5
Transitional Word
"Finally, the rain stopped."
Connects to what came before: However, Meanwhile, Then...

Tricky
Watch Out
Two important things to remember.
✏️ Don't Forget the Comma
When you start a sentence with an adverb, where/when phrase, describing phrase, or transitional word, you almost always need a comma before the main part of the sentence.
✓ "Carefully, she placed the vase on the shelf."
✗ "Carefully she placed the vase on the shelf."
⚖️ Variety Means a MIX
The goal is variety, not replacing one pattern with another. Don't fix "every sentence starts with She" by making every sentence start with an adverb. A good paragraph has a mix of different beginnings.
✗ "Quickly, she ran. Happily, she smiled. Eagerly, she waved." — Still repetitive.
✓ "She ran quickly. Happily, she smiled. At the gate, she waved." — Now that is variety.
